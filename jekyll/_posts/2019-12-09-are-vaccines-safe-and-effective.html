There's a lot of controversy concerning the safety and effectiveness of vaccines. The polarization around this issue is extreme, with one side believing vaccines are completely safe and represent humanity's greatest medical achievement, while the other side claims they are only marginally effective and carry with them extreme risks for autism, neurological disorders and autoimmune diseases.

Let's begin an exploration of the potential issues by first discussing what vaccines are, how they work, and the common ingredients.

What are Vaccines?

Vaccines are a form of medication containing dead or weakened versions of disease-causing microorganisms, such as bacteria or viruses, which triggers the immune system to learn how to fight off the illness before it becomes a problem.(1)

What is in Vaccines?

Many believe that the dead or weakened pathogen is essentially all there is to a vaccine, but there are many other ingredients thrown into the mix for various reasons. Let's take a look at a few of these.

Adjuvents

The dead or weakened versions of the target microorganisms are often unable to trigger the body's immune system enough on their own for the vaccine to work, so other ingredients called 'adjuvents' are added to the vaccine to boost the immune response.(2)

  • Aluminum(2) - A neurological and immune toxin which accumulates in the body's tissue. Linked to Alzheimer's Disease, seizures, depression, autoimmune issues, autism, neurological disorders, Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS) and cancer.(3,4,5,6,7,8,9,10,11)

    Vaccines containing aluminum have an average of 392mcg of this heavy metal per 0.5mL dose.(12) That's 31,360x the FDA's maximum safe dose of 25mcg Aluminum per Liter.(13)

  • Squalene(2) - An oil usually derived from shark livers. It's a oil-in-water preparation which is known to invoke strong immune reactions. Linked to Gulf War Syndrome from the Anthrax Vaccine.(14,15)

Preservatives

In order to prevent vaccines from spoiling while in development or storage, other ingredients are added to act as preservatives.

  • Mercury(16) - The 2nd most toxic element known to man. It's linked to cognitive difficulties, memory and vision loss, fatigue, depression, coordination issues, tremors, skin rashes, mood instability, Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, Autism, Lupus and Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis.(17,18,19,20,21). A single does of the influenza vaccine increases a 6 month old's blood mercury levels 39mcg per Liter of blood in their body, and the CDC states that 85mcg per Liter is associated with neurodevelopmental problems.(22,23)

  • Glutaraldehyde(24) - Poisonous if ingested, and causes birth defects in animal experiments. Linked to respiratory issues (e.g. asthma) and allergic reactions which worsen with repeated exposure.(25,26,27,28)

  • Formaldahyde(24) - Known to cause cancer in humans. Damages the respiratory, gastrointestinal, immune, liver, nerve and reproductive systems.(29,30,31,32,33,34,35)

Stabilizers

Vaccine formulations can change while sitting in storage due to exposure to heat, light, etc. Stabilizers prevent or slow this type of degradation.

  • Monosodium Glutamate (MSG)(24) - An excitotoxin linked to headaches, fatigue, disorientation, heart palpitations and neuron death. Autistic people tend to have high levels of glutamate.(9,36,37,38,39)

  • Polysorbate 80(24) - Known to cause reproductive issues and increase susceptibility to oxidative stress. Carries other toxic ingredients in vaccines across the blood brain barrier.(40,41,42)

Growth Medium

In order to be used in vaccines, microorganisms must first be grown in big batches. Here are a few of the ingredients used to host and feed them during this phase of production.

  • Aborted Human Fetal Tissue(43,44) - A concern for anyone who objects to abortion for moral reasons. There are also concerns about potential problems caused by injecting foreign DNA fragments into the bloodstream.(45,46)

  • Animal Blood & Organs(24) - A concern for anyone practicing a vegetarian or vegan lifestyle. Also carries the risk of transmitting diseases such as the cancer causing SV40.(47)

  • Egg Protein(24) - Possibly related to high rates of egg allergies in children since adjuvents cause the immune system to become reactive to all present foreign proteins.(48)

How Many Vaccines Do We Get?

The number of doses we receive is going to matter when we're talking about injecting potentially toxic materials into our bodies, especially for young children. Let's take a look at the Center for Disease Control's recommended vaccine schedule.

The First Year

Infants in the United States generally receive around 22 vaccine doses in their first year of life, more than any other nation, including the completely unnecessary Hepatitis B vaccine the day they are born.(49) These vaccines are formulated for children 6 years of age, which means these babies are receiving doses designed for someone multiple times their body weight.(50)

In the vaccine schedule chart above, taken directly from the Center for Disease Control's website, we can also see that many of the vaccines are administered at the same time, with 6 injections being given at just 2-months of age and 5 more at 4-months of age.(49) Is it surprising that the United States has the worst infant mortality rate of any developed nation?(51)

The First 18 Years

The toxic onslaught continues through the rest of childhood with a total of 26 more vaccine injections, including 17 of the mercury-laden influenza vaccine.(49,16)

Prior to 1986 children were only given 11 vaccine doses by their 18th birthday, but that number has now jumped to 48.(46,49) It's worth noting here that Chronic Illness in children was only 12.8% before 1986, and jumped to 54.6% by 2017.(52,53)

Is it a coincidence that both childhood vaccine doses and chronic illness rates both increased by 4.3x over the same period of time?

Governmental Regulatory Agencies

Just because doctors blindly trust the information they get regarding vaccine safety from other sources doesn't necessarily mean those other sources are wrong. So let's take a look at the governmental regulatory agencies giving doctors this information.

The FDA

The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) is the organization that evaluates and licenses drugs to be sold to the general population of the United States. You can think of them as the first line of defense in protecting the public from drugs which may cause harm.

Normally, a drug company is required to conduct multi-year safety studies before a drug is approved for sale by the FDA. However, in the case of vaccines the study period is often only a few days. For example, the Hepatitis B vaccine, which is given to infants on their first day of life, was licensed based on studies only 4-5 days in length, and Polio was licensed after only 2 days.(56,57,58)

Drug safety studies also normally require the use of an experimental group of people, the ones given the drug, and a control group given an inactive substance (i.e. placebo). After a period of time these two groups are compared statistically to see if the drug caused any increases in health problems. However, in the case of vaccines, the FDA allows studies to simply compare against another vaccine or a version of the test vaccine with only the microorganism element removed. This means that vaccines are licensed based on studies which compare the vaccine against injections containing all of the same adjuvents, preservatives and stabilizers discussed above.(46,59,60,61,62)

Considering vaccines are often administered 6 at a time in 2-month-olds, you would think there would be a requirement to conduct safety studies where vaccines are given in combination. Sadly, these tests are never done before a vaccine is licensed by the FDA.(63)

As if all of that weren't bad enough, the United States Congress investigated the FDA and found that a majority of the members on the committees approving vaccines have financial ties to the vaccine industry.(64) In other words, their goal is to sell as many vaccines as possible, not to protect the public.

The CDC

The Center for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) is responsible for monitoring the effect of a vaccine on the general population after it's been licensed by the FDA. They can be thought of as the second line of defense in ensuring vaccines aren't harming people.

A majority of the CDC's evaluation of vaccine safety is done through statistical evaluations of archived medical data. Unfortunately, it's very easy to manipulate data to show the results you want them to show. So the question becomes whether or not the CDC has a history of bias and an incentive to falsify data.

Let's take a look at the vaccine research known as the Danish Studies. The CDC hired the statistician Poul Thorsen to evaluate a large amount of medical records out of Denmark. While he was working on this analysis, he allegedly stole over a million dollars of grant money from the CDC, went into hiding and is currently on the FBI's Most Wanted List.(65,66) Yet, to this day his work is considered to be some the strongest evidence we have that vaccines are safe.

Dr. William Thompson, a researcher at the CDC, revealed that he had been instructed in a secret meeting in 2003 to destroy data which showed African American boys were 3.4 times more likely to develop autism if they received the MMR vaccine before they were 36-months old.(67) Congressman Bill Posey asked Congress to investigate the matter, but Dr. William Thompson has still not been subpoenaed several years later.(68)

In the year 2000, the CDC convened an emergency meeting in Simponwood Georgia to discuss how to cover-up results showing that there was a dose dependent reaction to Mercury in vaccines of up to 11 times the number of adverse neurological reactions in children. In other words, the more mercury a child received, the more likely they were to have serious health problems.(69) Still to this day the CDC recommends that everyone receive a yearly flu vaccine containing mercury.

Those are just two of the examples we know about of the CDC coming across results they didn't like and trying to hide them from the public, and there are plenty more examples.

To make matter worse, there are multiple instances of CDC employees accepting jobs at vaccine corporations upon leaving. One of these people is Julie Gerberdine, the Director of the CDC from 2002-2009, who took a job as the President of the Vaccine Division at Merck upon leaving the CDC, and made 2.34 Million Dollars selling 38,368 shares of Merck stock.(70,71) Considering the CDC is supposed to be policing the vaccine industry, this type of revolving door situation is a major red flag for collusion.

It's worth mentioning that the CDC holds several patents for vaccines, which means they receive money for every sale.(72) Also, the largest portion of the CDC's budget is for purchasing, promoting and selling vaccines.(46) In other words, their priority is the sale of vaccines, not the verification of their safety.

If the CDC really wanted to be sure that vaccines weren't causing any health problems, they would run a simple statistical analysis comparing vaccinated vs. unvaccinated children. The CDC has never done this type of analysis despite the The Institute of Medicine stating that they could easily do so using the medical records in the Vaccine Safety Datalink (VSD).(73) They also admit that they have never studied the long-term effects of receiving multiple vaccines at once.(46)

What are the Dangers of Vaccines?

As you can see, there are a lot of reasons to doubt we can trust the people assuring us vaccines are safe. Just because our watchdogs are sleeping on the job still doesn't mean vaccines aren't safe, so let's sample some of problems with vaccines we're able to learn from more independent sources.

Vaccine Safety Research

I'll start off by mentioning that Neil Z. Miller, a medical research journalist, has been looking into the vaccine issue for over 30 years.(83) He reports that there are 1000s of published peer-reviewed scientific articles which call vaccine safety into question.(54) He provides brief summaries of 400 of the most important of these in his book, "Miller's Review of Critical Vaccine Studies." When your doctor tells you there's no evidence vaccines are unsafe, you may want to point them towards this book.

The VAERS Database

Health and Human Services (HHS), the parent organization of the CDC and FDA, maintains a database containing all reported cases of vaccines causing harm called the Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS).(84) In 2016 alone there were 59,000 reported negative reactions to vaccines, including 10,000 emergency room visits, 1000 permanent disabilities and 432 deaths.(46)

For something that the regulatory agencies claim is safe, that is a lot of harm to be occurring in a single year. Even worse, it is well known that adverse events are underreported to the VAERS database, with estimates of less that 1% of events being reported.(85,86,85) 1000 life-long disabilities and 432 death per year seems like a lot to me, and the under-reporting estimates mean those could potentially be 100x higher than that.

The reason for under-reporting is that it's a manual process. Doctors are required by law to submit a report for every instance, but very few actually do. To solve this problem, the CDC worked with Harvard Pilgrim Health Care to create an automated reporting system using the medical records of their patients. The system ensured that a majority of adverse events were captured, and the results revealed that 2.6% of vaccine injections resulted in harm to the patient.(85) Considering the childhood vaccine schedule the CDC recommends includes 48 doses, every single child is practically guaranteed to experience at least one adverse event before they turn 18.

After having invested so much into testing the automated VAERS database, and it being such a success, you'd think the CDC would have immediately begun making efforts to implement the system nationwide. Unfortunately, in their report Harvard Pilgrim states that the CDC dropped the program and ceased responding to their repeated inquiries on how to proceed.(85) This is sadly consistent with the CDC's history of choosing to hide data unfavorable to vaccines from the public.

Vaccine Injury Court

Due to the high number of lawsuits vaccine manufacturers were experiencing, they notified the federal government that they would discontinue production unless something was done to protect them. In response, the National Childhood Vaccine Injury Act of 1986 was passed. This made the vaccine manufacturers immune to legal responsibility for harm caused, and created the National Vaccine Injury Compensation Program to provide financial support to victims who were no longer able to sue the corporations.(79,87)

This compensation program, often referred to as Vaccine Injury Court, provides no judge, jury, or legal discovery and does not allow the press to monitor its proceedings. Victims must prove to a "Special Master" that it was the vaccine which caused the damage using accepted scientific studies, the kind that are generally funded by the CDC and tend to leave out negative results. Nearly all cases are thrown out, and even if the Special Master decides compensation is appropriate, there is a cap of $250,000.(87,46).

Despite how difficult it is to prove your case and get compensation, the court has still paid out over $4,000,000,000 of taxpayer's money since its creation.(88)

The Austism Epidemic

While far from the only example of potential damage caused by vaccines, Autism is probably the most well known among the general public. The CDC tell us unequivocally that Vaccines do not cause Autism, so why is there so much concern about it?(89) To understand this, we must examine the history of Autism.

The disorder now known as Autism first appeared shortly after the introduction of Pertussis vaccine, and was described as a set of symptoms that the medical community had never seen before. In the early days it tended appear most in the children of wealthier parents, the kind of people more likely to be able to afford vaccination. When socioeconomic disparities began to disappear and vaccines became accessible to everyone, Autism began to more evenly effect all levels of society.(90)

Populations which tend not to vaccinate their children have extremely low to non-existent rates of Autism. For example, in 2005 an examination of an Amish community in Pennsylvania who tended not to vaccinate their children was done. Given the population and Autism rates at the time, there should have been over 100 children in their community with the disorder. Only 4 were found, and the element common to each of them was vaccination.(91)

Comparing Vaccinated & Unvaccinated Children

The Institute of Medicine says no large-scale studies have ever compared the health outcomes of entirely unvaccinated populations to vaccinated.(92,63) This is a result of the agencies with the financial ability to conduct such large and intensive studies refusing to do so.(46) However, we do have some smaller more independent studies which give us a sense for the results we'd see if the CDC would do its job.

For example, in 2017 there was a study of homeschoolers comparing the health outcomes of those who had been vaccinated and those who hadn't. The results showed that while vaccinated children were less likely to get the diseases the vaccines target, they were more likely to have the following ailments: Allergies (30x), Learning Disabilities (5x), Austism (4x), ADHD (4x), Neurological Disorders (4x) and Eczema (3x).(93)

Considering the ailments the vaccines aim to prevent are generally mild and temporary, while the other issues they appear to cause are fairly severe and last a lifetime, are the vaccines really worth it?

Do Vaccines work?

Now that we've discussed some of the potential hazards, lack of adequate safety studies, and instances of collusion and conflict of interest between the industry and governmental agencies, let's discuss the actual efficacy of the common vaccines at preventing the diseases they target so that we can evaluate whether or not they are worth the risks.

Flu Vaccine

In 2018-19 the Flu Vaccine was 29% effective for the strains it targeted, and only 14% effective for people above the age of 50. It was only 9% effective against the most common strain during this period of time.(105)

Polio Vaccine

The first Polio vaccine more than doubled infection rates after it was introduced in the 1950s. Before the vaccine was licensed Polio was already on the decline, with death rates dropping 50% between 1923 and 1953. Cases continued to drop drastically on the same decline they were experiencing before the vaccine, including in countries which opted not to vaccinate their citizens.(106)

Nearly all the cases of Polio in the United States between 1961 and 2000 were due to the live virus present in the vaccine itself which caused the recipient to spread the disease to others. There have been no cases of Polio since it was switched to an inactive virus version of the vaccine.(107,108)

Measles Vaccine

The incidence of Measles dropped drastically after the introduction of the vaccine.(109,110) However, by that time the severity of the illness had already decreased from thousands of deaths per million in the mid 1800s to as low as 1 death per million (far less dangerous than the common flu is today) in Scandinavia as a result of improved health and hygiene before the vaccine was even available.(54). In the United States the severity of the disease dropped by 95% between 1920 and 1960 before the vaccine was introduced.(111)

Outbreaks of Measles in recent years affected vaccinated people as well, usually making up a majority of those infected.(112,113,114)

Chickenpox Vaccine

The Chickenpox Vaccine has been shown to be about 90% effective over a period of about 14 years.(115,116) However, it nearly doubles the incidence of Shingles, an illness caused by the same virus. It also increases the incidence of Shingles in children, normally something which occurs only in adulthood, by 3-6 times. Considering the medical costs associated with Shingles are 3 times that of Chickenpox, the value of the vaccine should be called into question.(95)

Pertussis Vaccine

The Pertussis Vaccine protects against Whooping Cough at rates between 44% and 80%.(117) However, it's efficacy drops off very quickly over a short period requiring frequent re-vaccinations.(118,119) A majority of cases of Whooping Cough occur in vaccinated populations.(120,119) Despite over 50 years of vaccination, infection rates for Whooping Cough are on the rise.(121)

Mumps Vaccine

Estimates of Mumps Vaccine efficacy range anywhere from 37% to 85%.(122) Outbreaks of Mumps has been occurring regularly in vaccinated populations since the mid 1990s.(123) Also, two former Merck employees filed a lawsuit against the manufacturer of the vaccine stating that it had falsified data in order to make it appear more effective than it was, and that the low efficacy of the drug was known internally before licensing.(124)

HPV Vaccine

The Gardasil Vaccine targets 4 of the over 150 HPV strains. 2 of the strains it targets are known to cause cervical cancer in later life, but there are 13 other cancer causing strains it doesn't address.(99,62) There is no evidence that the vaccine prevents these types of cancers, or that any immunity it might provide lasts until the age of 50 when these types of cancers begin to appear.(125,62)

It is primarily administered to children between the ages of 9 and 18.(126) When administered to individuals already infected with HPV it can increase the severity of the condition, and can even infect some people with the virus who had never been exposed.(125,62)

What About Herd Immunity?

Herd Immunity is a term used by advocates of vaccines to justify forcing the unwilling to vaccinate themselves in order to protect the population at large. However, it's important to note that term has been repurposed from the original definition, and is actually impossible through the use of vaccines due to the short lifespan of immunity they provide.

True herd immunity is when a future mother gets infected and gains immunity naturally gives that immunity to her child in utero and through breast feeding. As that gifted immunity wanes it is boosted by older children in the community having the illness and giving the young child light exposure (much like a vaccination). The child will therefore get the illness with all of its symptoms at an older age when it is something their body can deal with, and in the case of a female start the cycle all over again. Vaccinations actually sabotage this cycle, and make it more likely that children get illnesses at younger more dangerous ages.(127,128)

Herd immunity through vaccination is an entirely different process. There are many reasons this doesn't work which are discussed in the article (e.g. vaccines are only marginally effective, diseases mutate and vaccines are too specific to deal with this, vaccine immunity is short-lived compared to immunity through natural exposure, etc.).(129,130,128) The main issue with advocating herd immunity through vaccination is that it requires one take the stance that you have the right to force medicate the unwilling, which is a violation of human rights, and has been used for bad purposes in the past.
